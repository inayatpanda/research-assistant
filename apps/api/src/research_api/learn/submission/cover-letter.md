## What

A cover letter is a short, one-page note to the editor that frames why your manuscript matters for THIS journal. It is the first thing the editor reads and the document most likely to trigger or avoid a desk-rejection.

## When

Write it last, after the manuscript is finalised. Re-write for every new journal you submit to — never reuse a previous submission's letter verbatim.

## How

A strong cover letter has five paragraphs:

1. Salutation and submission line: "Dear Dr. [Editor name]," and one sentence stating manuscript title, type (e.g. original research, systematic review), and journal section.
2. Significance: 2-3 sentences on the clinical or scientific problem and why your study advances it.
3. What you did: 2-3 sentences summarising design, key result, and the magnitude of the effect with one number (effect size + CI or p, not both).
4. Why this journal: 1-2 sentences linking your scope/audience to the journal's. Cite one or two recent papers from the journal you build on.
5. Boilerplate: declarations that the work is original, not under consideration elsewhere, all authors have approved, COI and funding are declared, and any preprint is disclosed. Sign off with the corresponding author's full contact details.

## Pitfalls

- Restating the abstract verbatim — wastes the editor's time.
- Hyperbolic claims ("groundbreaking", "first ever") not supported by the results.
- Omitting required disclosures (preprint server + DOI, related submissions, ethics approval number).
- Wrong editor name or wrong journal — instant credibility loss.

## Template

```
[Date]
Dr. [Editor first name] [Editor last name]
Editor-in-Chief, [Journal Name]

Dear Dr. [Last name],

We submit our manuscript, "[Full title]," as an [Article type] for consideration in [Journal section]. The work addresses [problem], which is important because [clinical or policy reason].

We [design summary: e.g. randomised 312 patients across 4 centres to compare X vs Y]. [Headline finding with one effect size and CI.] These findings [practical implication].

We believe this study fits [Journal] because [scope/audience match] and complements [Journal]'s recent work on [cite one paper, year].

All authors have approved this version; the work is original and is not under consideration elsewhere. [Preprint at bioRxiv DOI ...] Conflict-of-interest and funding statements appear in the manuscript. Please address correspondence to [Name, email, ORCID].

Yours sincerely,
[Corresponding author full name, qualifications]
```
