## What

A point-by-point document responding to every reviewer comment. The single most influential document in revision — strong responses can turn a marginal "major revision" into an acceptance.

## When

Begin within 48 hours of receiving the decision letter. Submit within the journal's revision deadline (usually 30-90 days).

## How — structure

1. A cover paragraph thanking the editor and reviewers, summarising the most important changes.
2. For each reviewer, reproduce each comment verbatim, then respond in two parts: (a) what you did, with line numbers in the revised manuscript; (b) why. Use a different colour or italic font for changes to the manuscript itself.

```
Reviewer 1, Comment 3
"The sample size justification is unclear."

Response. We have rewritten the sample-size paragraph (Methods, p.6, lines 142-156) to specify the assumed effect size (Cohen's d = 0.45), alpha (0.05, two-sided), power (0.80), and the resulting target of n = 80 per arm. This was the calculation in our pre-registered protocol (OSF DOI ...). We have also added a sentence on attrition assumptions.
```

3. Where you disagree, push back politely with evidence — cite literature, show the analysis you ran in response, and acknowledge the reviewer's concern. Never dismiss.
4. End with a summary table of all changes, mapped to manuscript line numbers.

## Tone

Be neither defensive nor servile. The aim is collaboration. "We thank Reviewer X for this insightful suggestion" is fine once or twice — overuse rings hollow.

## When to push back

- The reviewer asks for an analysis your data cannot support (e.g. subgroup with n=4) → explain the limitation and offer a sensitivity analysis instead.
- The reviewer asks for a citation to a non-relevant paper (possible self-citation request) → cite it only if it genuinely adds value, otherwise decline politely.
- The reviewer asks for a fundamental redesign your study cannot now adopt → acknowledge as a limitation and propose as future work.

## Pitfalls

- Silent edits: every change to the manuscript should be flagged in the response document.
- Aggregating responses ("We have addressed all of Reviewer 2's points") — editors expect line-by-line.
- Forgetting to update tables, figures, supplementary, abstract, and key-message bullets to match the new text.

## Template

Use a two-column table (or our app's `Response to reviewers` editor) — left column: comment + reviewer ID; right column: response + manuscript line ranges.
