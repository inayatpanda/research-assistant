## What

The International Committee of Medical Journal Editors (ICMJE) defines four criteria for authorship. To be an author, a contributor must meet ALL four; contributors who meet fewer should be acknowledged instead.

## The four ICMJE criteria

1. Substantial contributions to the conception or design of the work; OR the acquisition, analysis, or interpretation of data for the work.
2. Drafting the work or reviewing it critically for important intellectual content.
3. Final approval of the version to be published.
4. Agreement to be accountable for all aspects of the work in ensuring that questions related to the accuracy or integrity of any part of the work are appropriately investigated and resolved.

## When

Settle the authorship list at protocol stage. Re-confirm after the analysis is complete. Re-confirm again before submission — late additions invite rejection.

## How

- Maintain a contributor matrix capturing who did what (conception, data collection, analysis, writing, revising). Many journals require this in the cover letter or as a CRediT taxonomy table (14 roles, from Conceptualisation through Visualisation to Writing - Review & Editing).
- Designate one corresponding author (responsible for communication, COI, data sharing) and, where allowed, a guarantor (takes overall responsibility).
- Equal contribution: footnoted, and increasingly common to list two senior authors as co-last.

## Acknowledgement vs authorship

People who contributed but did not meet all four criteria — for example, a statistician who advised but did not draft, a study nurse who collected data, a department head who funded but did not review — belong in the Acknowledgements with explicit consent and a description of their role.

## Pitfalls

- "Gift" and "ghost" authorship: both violate ICMJE and most journals' policies. Gift = added but did not contribute; ghost = contributed but not listed.
- Authorship by seniority alone. Position in the department is not a criterion.
- Disputes after submission. Authorship-order changes after submission require a written agreement signed by all authors.
