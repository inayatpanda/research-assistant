## What

A conflict of interest (COI) is any relationship that could bias — or be perceived to bias — your work. Disclosure is mandatory at most journals via the ICMJE COI form (the unified electronic form, sometimes called "Disclosure of Interest").

## When

- At protocol stage: declare ongoing relationships in the trial registry record.
- At submission: each author completes the ICMJE form covering the 36 months preceding the work plus any ongoing relationships.
- At any time COIs change during peer review: update the editor in writing.

## How

The ICMJE form has four substantive sections:

1. Work under consideration: grants/payments to the author or their institution that supported THIS study.
2. Relevant financial activities outside the submitted work: consultancies, employment, stock, royalties, expert testimony, etc., over the previous 36 months.
3. Intellectual property: patents and copyrights.
4. Other relationships: anything else readers might consider relevant (e.g. unpaid leadership of a professional society funded by industry).

Authors complete the form themselves; the corresponding author should not fill in others' disclosures. Most journals embed an aggregated "Declaration of competing interests" statement at the end of the manuscript that mirrors the forms.

## Pitfalls

- "Nothing to declare" when the author is a paid consultant. Consultancies of even nominal value require declaration.
- Splitting disclosure across "personal" and "institutional" payments to obscure the magnitude — declare both.
- Omitting non-financial COIs (advocacy roles, unpublished opposing data).
- Forgetting to update the form between revisions.

## What journals do with it

Disclosed COIs do not automatically disqualify a paper but they may trigger additional editorial scrutiny, requests for independent statistical review, or transparency notes appended to the published article.

## Template

Standard manuscript statement:

> "[Authors A and B] report consultancy fees from [Company] outside the submitted work. [Author C] is supported by a [Funder] research training fellowship. The remaining authors declare no competing interests. The complete ICMJE Disclosure of Interest forms are submitted with this manuscript."
