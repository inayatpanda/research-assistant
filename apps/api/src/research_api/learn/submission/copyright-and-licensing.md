## What

When a journal accepts your paper you choose (or are assigned) one of three routes: closed access with copyright transferred to the publisher, hybrid open access with a Creative Commons licence (you pay an APC), or fully open access where the journal is OA by design.

## The three OA flavours

- Gold OA: published open access in the journal, usually in exchange for an article processing charge (APC). The version of record is freely available immediately under a CC licence.
- Green OA: closed at the publisher but you deposit the accepted manuscript ("post-print") in an institutional or subject repository (PMC, arXiv, your university repository) after the publisher's embargo (typically 6-12 months).
- Diamond OA: published OA with no APC. The journal is funded by societies, libraries, or universities. Growing in popularity for ethics and inclusion reasons.

## Creative Commons licences (quick reference)

- CC BY: attribution required, all reuse permitted including commercial and derivatives. Funders such as Wellcome, NIH (2026 default), cOAlition S strongly prefer this.
- CC BY-SA: as CC BY plus derivatives must use the same licence (share-alike).
- CC BY-NC: non-commercial reuse only — restricts industry and many AI training uses.
- CC BY-NC-ND: no commercial use and no derivative works — most restrictive of the CC suite; many funders will not accept it for funded research.
- Copyright transferred to publisher: classical closed-access route; you retain limited rights (often only personal/teaching use).

## When

Make the decision before signing the publication agreement. Funder mandates (Plan S, Wellcome, NIH Public Access Policy, UKRI policy) often dictate the licence — check your funder portal before clicking Accept.

## Pitfalls

- Signing an exclusive copyright transfer that conflicts with funder mandates. Use a Rights Retention Statement at submission ("This research was funded in whole or in part by [Funder]. For the purposes of open access, the author has applied a CC BY public copyright licence to any author accepted manuscript arising from this submission.").
- Posting the publisher PDF (version of record) on ResearchGate or your website when the agreement only allowed the accepted manuscript. Most publishers' permissions are repository-only, not personal-website.
- Choosing CC BY-NC-ND because it "feels safer" — it blocks legitimate translations and text-and-data-mining.
- APC waivers exist for low- and middle-income-country authors and for hardship — ask before paying.

## Template

When the publisher offers a CTA but your funder requires CC BY, attach a Rights Retention Statement to your submission and update the manuscript footer accordingly. Most major publishers will accommodate it once you point to the funder policy URL.
