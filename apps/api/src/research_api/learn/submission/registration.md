## What

Prospective registration deposits your study's research question, design, and pre-specified analyses in a public registry BEFORE you start data collection or analysis. Registration is a prerequisite for ICMJE-member journals to consider a clinical trial.

## Where (by study type)

- Clinical trials: ClinicalTrials.gov (USA, global), ISRCTN (UK/global), ANZCTR (Australia/NZ), EU Clinical Trials Register (EU CTR), or any other WHO Primary Registry. Required by ICMJE for interventional trials before first participant is enrolled.
- Systematic reviews: PROSPERO (most common, international), OSF Registries, or the Cochrane registry for Cochrane reviews. Recommended before screening begins.
- Observational studies: OSF Registries, AsPredicted, or the Registry of Patient Registries. Encouraged by STROBE.
- Preclinical experiments: PreclinicalTrials.eu, OSF Registries. Encouraged by ARRIVE 2.0 item 19.
- Secondary data analyses: OSF Registries, AsPredicted — useful for distinguishing pre-specified from exploratory analyses.

## When

Before the first participant is enrolled (trials), before screening starts (SRs), before data are seen (secondary analyses). Retrospective registration is allowed in some registries but is flagged as such and may exclude the work from many journals.

## How

1. Draft the protocol (PICO/PICOS for SRs, full SAP for trials).
2. Open an account on the appropriate registry. Provide details: title, design, primary and secondary outcomes, intervention, eligibility criteria, sample size, statistical methods, timeline.
3. Address registry curation feedback (PROSPERO and ClinicalTrials.gov both review submissions; expect 1-4 weeks).
4. Capture the registration ID in your protocol, ethics application, manuscript, and CONSORT/PRISMA item 24a.

## Why

- Reduces publication bias and selective outcome reporting.
- Stakes priority on the research question.
- Required by ICMJE, most funders (NIH, MRC, NIHR), and IRBs/RECs for interventional research.

## Pitfalls

- Registering after enrolment ("retrospective"). Most ICMJE journals will refuse such trials.
- Outcome switching: silently changing the registered primary outcome between protocol and manuscript. COMPare and other monitoring projects flag this publicly.
- PROSPERO records that are vague ("usual outcomes will be assessed") — defeats the purpose. Be specific.

## Template

In your manuscript methods: "This [trial / systematic review] was prospectively registered with [Registry name] on [date] (registration number [NCT/ISRCTN/CRD]). The protocol is publicly available at [URL]."
