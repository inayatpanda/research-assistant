# CARE reporting checklist (13 items)

## Scope

CARE is for single-patient case reports — diagnostic puzzles, unusual presentations, adverse-event narratives, or first-in-human use of a therapy. For three or more patients use a case series guideline (PROCESS for surgery, SCARE for surgical adverse events). CARE asks for explicit patient perspective and a visual timeline.

## Key items the user must report

- [ ] 1. Title: the words "case report" should appear in the title along with the area of focus.
- [ ] 2. Key words: 2-5 key words that identify areas covered in this case report.
- [ ] 3. Abstract: a structured abstract — introduction, patient information, diagnosis, intervention, outcomes, and conclusion.
- [ ] 4. Introduction: brief background summary of this case referencing the relevant medical literature.
- [ ] 5. Patient information: de-identified demographic information; primary concerns and symptoms of the patient; medical, family, and psychosocial history including diet, lifestyle, and genetic information; relevant past interventions and their outcomes.
- [ ] 6. Clinical findings: describe the relevant physical examination and other significant clinical findings.
- [ ] 7. Timeline: historical and current information from this episode of care organised as a timeline (figure or table).
- [ ] 8. Diagnostic assessment: diagnostic methods (such as PE, laboratory testing, imaging, surveys); diagnostic challenges (such as access to testing, financial, or cultural); diagnostic reasoning including other diagnoses considered; prognostic characteristics (such as staging) where applicable.
- [ ] 9. Therapeutic intervention: types of intervention (pharmacological, surgical, preventive, self-care); administration of intervention (dose, strength, duration); changes in intervention with explanation.
- [ ] 10a. Follow-up and outcomes: clinician- and patient-assessed outcomes when appropriate.
- [ ] 10b. Important follow-up diagnostic and other test results.
- [ ] 10c. Intervention adherence and tolerability (how was this assessed?).
- [ ] 10d. Adverse and unanticipated events.
- [ ] 11a. Discussion: a scientific discussion of the strengths AND limitations associated with this case report.
- [ ] 11b. Discussion of the relevant medical literature with references.
- [ ] 11c. The scientific rationale for any conclusions (including assessment of possible causes).
- [ ] 11d. The primary "take-away" lessons of this case report (without inappropriate generalisation).
- [ ] 12. Patient perspective: the patient should share their perspective on the treatment(s) received.
- [ ] 13. Informed consent: did the patient give informed consent? Please provide if requested.

## Common mistakes

- Omitting the timeline figure (item 7) — many journals will desk-reject without it.
- No patient perspective (item 12); this is a defining feature of CARE.
- Generalising from n=1 to population-level claims in the discussion.
- Stating "consent obtained" without specifying written informed consent and willingness to provide it.

## How this maps to manuscript sections in our app

- Manuscript -> Insert -> Timeline (table snippet) covers item 7.
- Frontmatter -> Ethics & consent field covers item 13.
- Frontmatter -> Patient perspective slot covers item 12.
